HR Hiring and Screening AI Policy Ensures AI-driven hiring follows bias and transparency rules. Owner: Chief People Officer Purpose Protect candidates and comply with employment regulations by governing AI tools used in recruiting, screening, assessments, and performance evaluations. Scope Covers automated resume screening, video interview analysis, assessments, ranking/recommendation tools, and any AI influencing employment decisions. Resume parsing and scoring tools Automated video interview analyzers AI-based assessments or chatbots interacting with candidates Internal tools ranking employees for promotions Definitions Automated Employment Decision Tool (AEDT): Any algorithm that substantially assists hiring or promotion decisions. Bias Audit: Independent assessment measuring disparate impact across protected classes. Candidate Notice: Communication informing applicants when AI is used and how to request alternatives. Policy All AEDTs must undergo annual bias audits, provide candidate notices, and offer human review options. HR must document purpose, data sources, and governance controls before deploying AI tools. Roles and Responsibilities Chief People Officer sponsors the program. HR Operations manages tool inventory and candidate notices. Responsible AI and Legal coordinate bias audits and regulatory compliance. Talent Acquisition ensures human oversight. Procedures HR AI workflow includes: Register AEDTs with purpose, data inputs, decision outputs. Conduct independent bias audits annually; publish summary as required. Provide candidate notices and alternative process instructions. Maintain human-in-the-loop review for adverse decisions. Log complaints and incident reports; escalate to HR leadership. Review vendors for compliance with local laws (e.g., NYC LL144). Exceptions Manual tools (e.g., keyword filters without automated scoring) may be exempt with Legal approval but must still provide transparency notices. Review Cadence Inventory, bias audit results, and candidate feedback reviewed quarterly. Policy updated when new jurisdictions enact AEDT laws. References NYC Local Law 144 (Automated Employment Decision Tools) EEOC guidance on AI in hiring Internal documents: AEDT Register, Bias Audit SOP, Candidate Notice Library
