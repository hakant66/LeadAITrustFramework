AI Regulatory Compliance Policy Maps internal obligations to EU AI Act, ISO 42001, and NIST AI RMF. Owner: Compliance Officer Purpose Provide a single policy framework for tracking AI regulatory requirements, assigning owners, and ensuring evidence is audit-ready for regulators, customers, and certification bodies. Scope Covers all AI systems governed by external regulations (EU AI Act, GDPR, sector-specific rules) or contractual obligations (customer audits, certifications). High-risk EU AI Act systems ISO/IEC 42001 AI Management System scope Customer or partner contractual commitments Regional privacy/security regulations impacting AI data Definitions Requirement Register: Living document mapping regulatory clauses to internal controls and owners. Evidence Library: Repository storing artifacts (policies, logs, reports) proving compliance. Control Owner: Person accountable for maintaining control effectiveness and documentation. Policy All applicable regulatory and contractual requirements must be captured in the register with control mappings, status, and evidence pointers. Control owners must update evidence quarterly or when significant changes occur. Compliance Officer coordinates regulator interactions and ensures timely submissions. Roles and Responsibilities Compliance Officer maintains the register, coordinates audits, and escalates gaps. Control Owners produce evidence and remediate findings. Legal monitors regulatory updates. Responsible AI ensures ethical requirements are represented. Procedures Compliance workflow includes: Requirement intake and classification (regulation, clause, applicability). Control mapping and assignment of owners with due dates. Evidence collection and storage in the library with retention tags. Gap analysis and remediation plans tracked to completion. Audit readiness checks prior to regulator/customer engagements. Change management when new regulations are published. Exceptions Temporary deferral of controls requires Compliance Officer approval with compensating controls and remediation deadlines. Review Cadence Requirement register is reviewed monthly; evidence completeness is sampled quarterly. Annual compliance report presented to the executive risk committee. References EU AI Act Articles 16–29, 61 (Obligations and monitoring) ISO/IEC 42001:2023 Clauses 9–10 (Performance evaluation, improvement) Internal documents: Compliance Register SOP, Evidence Management Playbook, Regulator Engagement Plan
