AI Governance Policy Umbrella policy establishing the AI governance council, lifecycle gates, and oversight routines. Owner: Chief AI Officer Purpose Defines how the organization evaluates, selects, procures, and approves AI systems and services so every purchase is trustworthy, explainable, controllable, and auditable. Ensures risk is minimized, regulations are met, and only fit-for-purpose vendors are activated. Scope Applies to the following AI-related activities and stakeholders. All third-party AI vendors engaged by the organization. External APIs providing AI inference or decisioning. Open-source or commercial model usage hosted internally or externally. Consulting services that deliver AI development or model hosting. Any internal team procuring or experimenting with AI capabilities on behalf of the organization. Guiding Principles We prioritize secure, privacy-preserving AI and align every decision with applicable regulatory frameworks and local laws. Evaluate alignment with EU AI Act, ISO 42001, ISO 27001, NIST AI RMF, and jurisdiction-specific laws when relevant. Favor vendors that provide transparency, robust controls, and demonstrable incident readiness. Requirements Before Purchase Each AI procurement request must include the following assessment criteria. Risk classification for the AI use case (High, Medium, Low). Vendor security posture and certifications (e.g., SOC 2, ISO 27001). Model transparency data such as model family, training disclosures, release notes, and version history. Privacy impact analysis covering the data that will be transmitted or stored. Incident reporting expectations, SLAs, and escalation paths. Ability to export, delete, or port data without undue burden. Capability to disable the service or switch vendors with reasonable effort. Inventory of vendor sub-processors and data residency commitments. Mandatory compliance review before purchase if the use case is classified as High Risk or operates in a regulated domain. Procurement Review Steps Requests follow this gated review before any contract is signed or activated. Business justification and risk classification submitted to the AI Governance intake. Vendor risk review and security questionnaire completed by Security or Vendor Risk. Legal review of terms, privacy policy, and data processing agreement. Technical feasibility, performance expectations, and reliability posture validated by Engineering. High-risk systems require sign-off from the CISO or AI Governance Lead. Procurement executes contracts only after all checkpoints are completed and documented. Performance &amp; Safety Evaluation Before production use the requesting team must: Run test evaluations with representative datasets or prompts. Benchmark hallucination, bias, and safety risks relative to risk tolerance. Document all findings in the AI risk register alongside mitigation actions. Monitoring After Procurement Approved AI systems require continuous oversight for the following signals. Performance drift or quality degradation versus agreed metrics. New regulatory requirements and contract term changes. Vendor security posture updates, incident disclosures, or sub-processor changes. Any unacceptable risk triggers immediate escalation and potential suspension. Exceptions Exception requests must be escalated to the AI Governance Lead with justification, compensating controls, and an expiration date. Executive leadership must approve the exception; individual contributors cannot bypass review. Enforcement Violations can result in procurement cancellation, vendor suspension, or disciplinary action proportional to the exposure created. The organization reserves the right to terminate vendor relationships that introduce unacceptable risk or breach contractual obligations.
