AI Transparency and User Notice Policy Specifies when and how users are informed about AI involvement. Owner: Product Lead Purpose Provide clear, consistent user disclosures about AI usage, explainability options, and human oversight so customers can make informed decisions. Scope Applies to any interface where users interact with AI-generated outputs or where AI influences decisions affecting individuals (e.g., recommendations, risk scores, HR tools). Chatbots, virtual assistants, and conversational agents Automated decision notices (loans, hiring, support) Content generation (emails, marketing copy) visible to customers Definitions Transparency Notice: User-facing message disclosing AI involvement, limitations, and escalation channels. Explainability Summary: Plain-language description of factors influencing automated decisions. Human-in-the-loop: Mechanism allowing users to request human review. Policy All applicable interfaces must display transparency notices at first interaction and provide access to explainability summaries or human review processes as required by law or internal policy. Roles and Responsibilities Product Lead ensures transparency is built into UX. Legal/Compliance review notices for regulatory accuracy. Responsible AI defines minimum disclosure content. Support teams manage escalation workflows. Procedures Transparency implementation includes: Identify touchpoints where AI outputs affect users. Select appropriate notice templates (inline banner, modal, tooltip). Provide explainability summary or factors used in automated decisions. Offer contact channel for human review or appeal. Log disclosure acceptance where regulations require evidence. Reassess notices whenever model behaviour changes materially. Exceptions Low-risk internal tools with no external user impact may be exempt with Responsible AI approval, but must document rationale. Review Cadence Transparency notices reviewed semi-annually and after regulatory updates to ensure accuracy. References EU AI Act Article 52 (Transparency obligations) GDPR Articles 13-15 (Information to data subjects) Internal documents: Transparency Notice Library, Explainability Playbook, Escalation SOP
